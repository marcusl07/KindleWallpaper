## Phase 14 — Wallpaper Restore Reliability

**T64c — Handle display reconfiguration with debounced restore**
Extend `DisplayTopologyCoordinator.swift` and `KindleWallApp.swift` to observe display-configuration changes in addition to wake events, then debounce event bursts into a single restore pass after topology settles. Cover wake-then-reconfigure and repeated reconfiguration sequences in `scripts/verify_t64.sh` / `scripts/verify_t64_main.swift`.

**T64e — Extract display identity and remapping heuristics**
Create `DisplayIdentityResolver.swift` and move display matching/remapping policy out of `WallpaperSetter.swift`, keeping `WallpaperSetter` focused on applying assignments. Wire `AppState.swift` to use the resolver for restore matching, and add adversarial tests for main-display changes, reordering, same-resolution displays, missing displays, and extra displays.

**T64f — Prevent transient reduced topologies from erasing full assignments**
Update `AppState.swift`, `ScheduleSettings.swift`, and `DisplayTopologyCoordinator.swift` so a temporary one-display snapshot does not immediately replace a previously saved two-display assignment set. Add integration coverage in `scripts/verify_t64.sh` / `scripts/verify_t64_main.swift` for two displays -> transient one display -> reconnect -> successful restore without losing the second assignment.

**T64g — Add no-op apply guards and final restore cleanup**
Tighten `DisplayTopologyCoordinator.swift`, `WallpaperSetter.swift`, and `KindleWallApp.swift` to skip redundant restore/apply cycles when the desired assignment is already active, and remove obsolete wake-specific glue left behind by earlier slices. Finish `scripts/verify_t64.sh` / `scripts/verify_t64_main.swift` with end-to-end coverage for the full wake/reconfigure/reconnect flow.
